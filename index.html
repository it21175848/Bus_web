Abstract

The Voice-Enhanced Smart Vehicle Transportation and Alerts Management System aims to modernize Sri Lanka’s public transportation by addressing critical issues such as unreliable schedules, long waiting times, and safety concerns for school children. The current lack of real-time tracking for buses and school vans leads to passenger inconvenience and inefficiencies, discouraging public transport use. Additionally, the absence of accessibility features for visually impaired users and secure payment solutions creates barriers for diverse user groups. The proposed system integrates real-time tracking and ETA notifications through a user-friendly mobile app, reducing waiting times and improving service reliability. Voice commands and sound alerts enhance accessibility for visually impaired users, enabling them to receive essential travel information. To modernize fare payments, the system features a re-loadable smart card integrated with a bitcoin-based payment platform, ensuring secure, contactless transactions. High-resolution cameras and Optical Character Recognition (OCR) enable vehicle identification, allowing users to verify vehicles and receive alerts on unauthorized movements, enhancing safety. Machine learning algorithms improve ETA predictions by analyzing traffic conditions, historical data, and route patterns, providing accurate and timely updates. A robust notification system alerts users about approaching buses, schedule changes, or delays, ensuring efficient trip planning. By prioritizing user convenience, safety, and inclusiveness, this system modernizes Sri Lanka’s public transport, making it more accessible, reliable, and secure. It sets a new standard for future advancements in the sector.

Literature Review

A review of technological advancements that shape modern public transport solutions, focusing on smart ticketing, tracking, and accessibility.

Public buses remain the most widely used form of public transportation in Sri Lanka, but the system is plagued by challenges such as inefficiency, inconsistent schedules, and poor accessibility [1]. While many global cities have modernized their transit with AI and IoT, Sri Lanka’s public transport is yet to benefit from such unified digital transformations.

ETA prediction and chatbot integration have become standard in international transport apps. Using Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) and machine learning models, accurate real-time ETAs can be generated by combining live traffic and historical data [2]. Furthermore, NLP-powered chatbots offer user-friendly, conversational interfaces that help commuters find route and schedule details efficiently, especially beneficial for tourists or first-time users [3].

Voice-enabled features greatly enhance inclusivity. NLP and text-to-speech technologies allow visually impaired users to navigate transit apps via voice commands, removing the dependency on visual UI elements [4]. These technologies are underused in local systems, leaving a critical gap in accessibility.

IoT-based modules offer safety and operational enhancements by alerting drivers about proximity to other buses or pedestrians. Studies suggest that onboard displays and inter-vehicle sensors reduce risks like dangerous overtaking and improve awareness at crowded bus stops [5]. This makes the transport environment safer for both passengers and operators [6].

NFC and blockchain integration represents a secure and efficient alternative to paper-based tickets. NFC enables fast, tap-to-pay transactions, while blockchain secures each transaction in a tamper-proof ledger. The combination offers a trustworthy, fraud-resistant solution that enhances user confidence [7][8]. Yet, most existing solutions deploy these technologies separately, not as a combined framework.

Seat availability tracking and smart recommendations are also gaining attention. Instead of expensive sensors, camera-based systems use ML to identify available seats in real time [9]. When paired with location-based services, passengers can receive suggestions about less crowded buses or nearby attractions—creating a more personalized journey experience [10].

While each of these innovations AI prediction, NFC-ticketing, IoT monitoring, and accessibility—has been individually explored in past research, a fully integrated solution designed specifically for Sri Lanka’s needs is lacking. This project addresses that gap by merging these technologies into one inclusive, intelligent, and scalable bus navigation platform.

Research Gap

Critical gaps in Sri Lanka's public transport systems that our integrated solution aims to address.

Inaccurate ETA Predictions

Current systems fail to accurately predict bus arrival times due to lack of integration between real-time and historical data sources.

Poor Accessibility

Public transport apps rarely support voice guidance or screen reader compatibility, making them unusable for visually impaired users.

No Intelligent Chatbot

Most apps lack conversational interfaces to help users ask for routes or bus details naturally, limiting usability for new users and tourists.

Lack of IoT-Based Alerts

There are no systems that provide real-time proximity alerts to drivers, leading to safety risks and reckless overtaking behaviors.

Fragmented Payment Systems

NFC and blockchain are not yet integrated into a single secure platform for ticketing, resulting in inefficiencies and trust issues.

No Smart Seat Availability

Current buses do not provide real-time seat availability. Existing solutions depend on costly IoT sensors instead of smart vision models.

Missing Travel Recommendations

There is no AI-powered recommendation system to guide passengers toward alternative routes or nearby points of interest.

Research Problem & Solution

Proposed Problem

How can we improve the accuracy, safety, and inclusivity of Sri Lanka’s bus transport system using modern technology?

The Sri Lankan public bus system faces critical challenges—manual ticketing, unpredictable arrivals, and lack of accessibility. Passengers often deal with delays, unsafe overtaking, and unfair fare collection. Visually impaired users and tourists struggle to access real-time information or navigate the system. Furthermore, there is no unified solution for digital payments, ETA prediction, or driver alerts. These issues reduce reliability, efficiency, and safety across the system.

Proposed Solution

Our proposed solution is an intelligent, mobile-integrated bus navigation and management system that combines: NFC card-based ticketing secured with blockchain, real-time bus tracking and ETA predictionvoice-enabled chatbotIoT module

NFC cards allow fast, contactless fare payment. Blockchain ensures secure, tamper-proof ticket validation. A real-time GPS tracking system paired with LSTM models improves ETA predictions under dynamic conditions. Meanwhile, onboard IoT displays warn drivers of nearby buses to reduce reckless overtaking. An AI chatbot supports conversational route queries and assists visually impaired users through voice navigation. Passengers also receive recommendations for available seats and nearby landmarks, improving comfort and experience. All data is presented on an accessible mobile dashboard for users and operators.

Methodology

A structured and modular approach was followed to implement the smart transport system with four major integrated components.

Figure: High-Level Architecture of the System

The system is composed of four main modules that work together to create a unified, intelligent public transport experience in Sri Lanka:

  1. ETA Prediction & Chatbot: Machine learning models like LSTM are trained on historical and real-time GPS data to predict accurate arrival times. An AI chatbot assists users with natural language queries about routes, schedules, and bus stops.
  2. IoT-Based Driver Alerts: Distance sensors are installed on buses to detect proximity of other buses and passengers at bus stops. These alerts are displayed on onboard screens to reduce overtaking risks.
  3. NFC + Blockchain Ticketing: A contactless payment system allows passengers to tap NFC cards. Each transaction is securely recorded using a blockchain ledger for transparency and fraud prevention.
  4. Seat Availability & Travel Recommendations: A camera-based seat monitoring module uses computer vision to detect available seats. The mobile app recommends comfortable options and nearby points of interest using location data.

The system follows the Agile development model and was deployed in phases. All modules communicate through a central backend and data is stored in a secure cloud environment. Notifications, ETA updates, and seat data are delivered through a unified mobile app interface.
